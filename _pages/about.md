I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Engineering at the University of California, San Diego, advised by Prof. Mingu Kang in the Vertically-integrated VLSI Information Processing (VVIP) Lab. My research focuses on hardware-algorithm co-design for efficient AI systems, with an emphasis on LLM optimization, in-memory computing, VLSI, and computer architecture.

I work across the stack, from compression and scheduling algorithms for Transformer inference to architecture, circuit design, and fabricated accelerator prototypes. Recent projects include low-rank KV cache compression, sparse attention acceleration, charge-based SRAM compute-in-memory, and PIM-enabled systems for generative models.

I received my M.S. in Computer Engineering from UC San Diego and my B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Tehran. In summer 2024, I was a Technology Research Intern at Intel, working on hardware accelerator design for large language models.

**Research interests:** hardware-algorithm co-design, LLM optimization, in-memory computing, VLSI, and computer architecture.
